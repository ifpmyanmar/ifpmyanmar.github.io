Our Activities

IFP Myanmar 2018 Activities Description

Our conference entails over 100 activities from 6 days.

Day 1

Team Bonding & Trust Building Activities This activity will serve as an icebreaking day to all the delegates and organizers and this day activities will give a foundational understanding of why this conference is being done and why it is needed for our community.

Day 2

Leadership and Empowerment Activities. This day will help the delegates to foster a sense of self-awareness and effectively empower them to become confident leaders. All activities for this day are planned in such a way that the delegates will. become trained, inspired and informed to morph into true change-makers. At the end of this day, the delegates will become more prepared to speak up and to make decisions, especially for marginalized people.

Day 3

Diversity and Identity Activities. This day activity will allow the delegates to dig deeper into themselves as they manage to explore what they are good at and what they need to improve on to become change-makers. These activities will bring out the true selves of all delegates and the people who have been feeling insignificant will finally have a chance to confide in their fellow delegates and ask for consultancy. This will create a better bond among all the delegates, regardless of everything else and at the end of this day, the delegates will be equipped with confidence, self-awareness, self-esteem and empathy for others.

Day 4

Peace and Conflict Activities This is the climax of our conference, the delegates will be able to engage in a heartwarming service day at Bago where they will first apply what they have learnt within the first three days of the conference. The delegates will be split into groups and they will deliver a mini-workshop and education session to monastic children and workers at a monastic school in Bago. The delegates will finally be able to witness what they can do to empower others even within the first few days of their conference experience. This will serve as a source of confidence to all of their future endeavours to come.

Day 5

Community Building Activities - The delegates will be given a hands-on experience and a simulation on how to build a sustainable community on this day. The delegates will be allowed to engage in rigorous discussion and presentation activities. The delegates will be encouraged to undergo ideation and implementation to prepare them to start designing their own community projects.

Day 6

Project Designing Activities and Farewell - This is the last day of the conference and the delegates will finally engage in an exciting yet most significant part of the conference. The delegates will be split into 6 groups who will all be given a short presentation on how to design a project or a business model after which the delegates will be allowed an hour to start designing their desired project. They will then be allowed 10 minutes each to pitch these projects and after the conference, these ideas will become the actual follow-up projects. So our conference will be spawning at least another 6 follow-up impacts that will then branch out into definitely bigger causes that are worth supporting. IfP Myanmar 2018 conference will then end victoriously and impactfully on 1pm sharp.

Activities Benefits

Day 1

activities will benefit the delegates who have not ever been into a medium crowd or a networking session. The delegates will foster self-confidence and this is especially an effective day for the delegates who have been marginalized due to their social context since everybody will be seen as equals and everybody will become a big family on the days onwards.

Day 2

activities will especially benefit the delegates who have been feeling significant at some point in their lives due to either self-doubts or socio-economic background because the activities on this day will allow the delegates to empower and inspire each other, leading all of them to become fully able to share their inspiring stories. This day is also very important because the delegates will be able to identify their leadership capacity that will be put to use in their future.

Day 3

activities will benefit people with unique identities like the women from oppressed communities, people from underprivileged backgrounds and individuals with different sexual orientations (LGBTQ) because on this day, the conference will serve as a positive and common ground for all people who can bravely step up and share about their stories and seek out for comfort and encouragement. This day will be the day when the delegates start to feel truthful and proud of themselves for being who they are and use their unique identities as a leverage to inspire and benefit other people after the conference.

Day 4

will be the day when the delegates will eventually feel that they can make an actual impact in the community. The pride and the satisfaction they will feel from the service day introduced on this particular day will be like nothing else. This is also the day when the delegates will meet other beneficiaries and deliver workshops for them. So this is going to be the first step for the delegates, including those marginalized or treated differently, to realize that they also can make changes as equally as everybody else.

Day 5

activities will allow the delegates to reflect about their own communities they grew up or live in and imagine the changes they can make for these communities. This is a day that can mean a huge impact not just for the delegates but also for the communities because the delegates are ready to visualize their own societies and identify the challenges that these societies face and brainstorm the solutions to them. So in short, these activities are very beneficial to those who want to make impactful changes but are never given a chance to even think about it because of being marginalized.

Day 6

activities will finally bid farewell to the delegates as they take away loads of knowledge and experiences about how to come the real community leaders. By the time the delegates leave the conference room on this final day, they will feel the energy to make changes in their own communities and people who think they are marginalized will become empowered more than ever. They will start to evolve into courageous leaders who will act differently and powerfully to make changes in various regions.
